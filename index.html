I am a researcher in the Applied Zero Knowledge Group at Etheruem Foundation. I work in the field of Cryptography. My interests in particular include Secure Multiparty Computation, Zer Knowledge Proofs and Homomorphic Encryption in practice. I have gained a special interest towards developing efficient techniques to solve problems in the domain of secure set operations like intersection, union, etc. I am also a consultant researcher at Chain Reaction, Israel. I finished my undergraduation at BITS Pilani, Goa with a double major in Math and Electronics.

I completed my senior thesis at The Center for Research in Applied Cryptography and Cyber Security, Bar Ilan Univeristy supervised by Prof. Carmit Hazay and Prof. Muthu Venkitasubramaniam, where we worked on an efficient solution for the PSI-Sum Problem. Recently, I interned at QED-it as a cryptography intern under Daniel Benarroch. and with the Blockchain and Smart Contracts group at IBM Research India Labs under Dr. Dhinakaran Vinayagamurthy.

I am mentoring students for PhD/MS/Job applications in CS Theory/Cryptography as a part of a remote mentorship program setup by my friend Khyati to raise funds for COVID relief efforts in India! Feel free to check out the website if you need mentoring. There are amazing mentors covering various domains.

Feel free to check out my CV and drop an e-mail if you want to chat with me! I am also planning to blog here.
